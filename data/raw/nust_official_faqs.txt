NUST Admissions - Official Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
Source: https://nust.edu.pk/faqs/

Q: Are there any quota / reserved seats?
A: There are no quota seats.

Q: Are there any reserved / quota seats for admission in MBBS Programme?
A: Admissions in NSHS will be purely onmerit, based upon theMDCATconducted byNUMS.

Q: Can a candidate apply both for open merit and ACT based seats?
A: Yes, on the basis of NET / MDCAT Score and ACT Composite Score.

Q: Can a candidate of ICS / equivalent qualification eligible to apply for Engineering programmes?
A: Yes, as per PEC regulation No PEC/EAD/46-VCCM/Regulations/2023 dated 30 March 2023, candidates having combination ofPhysics, MathematicsandComputer Studies / Computer Science (ICS) or equivalent are eligible to apply for all the Engineering programsoffered by NUST, but they have to clear Chemistry as a remedial subject / course in the 1st Semester after admission.Candidates, who already appeared in any NET series during current admission cycle, will also be able to update their choice preferences while applying for Series-4 or after the announcement of merit, before start of the selection process.

Q: Can a candidate with FSc Pre-Medical / equivalent qualification, appeared in additional mathematics paper and waiting for the result, apply for admission in Engineering programs on the basis of Pre Medical marks?
A: Candidate can apply on the basis of Pre-Medical result, however he / she has to provide the additional math result with passing marks and overall 60% marks in HSSC at the time of joining NUST.

Q: Can candidates of FSc Pre-Medical / equivalent qualification apply for BS Computer Science?
A: Yes, but they have to clear deficient Mathematics courses of 6 credit hours, within one year of enrollment in the programme.

Q: Can foreign / international students apply for admission in BSHND programme?
A: Foreign students are eligible to apply on the basis of NET-Applied Sciences / NUMS-MDCAT / National MDCAT / ACT Test.

Q: Can foreign students apply for admission at NSHS?
A: Foreign students can apply in NSHS on the basis of NUMS MDCAT, SAT-II or foreign MCAT / UCAT exam.

Q: Can I apply for computing programmes with HSSC / A level background in Arts / Humanities group?
A: Yes, any candidate who hasMathematicsas one of the mandatory subjects in HSSC / A level / Equivalent, is eligible to apply for computing programmes.

Q: Can I apply for more than one programme?
A: Yes, if you meet the eligibility criteria.

Q: Can I apply for rechecking of Entry Test Result?
A: Re-checking ofPaper BasedEntry Test may be requested within5 daysof the declaration of result along with a fee ofRs.500/-in the form of a bank draft/pay order in favour of NUST. Theoriginal bank draft / pay ordershould bedispatchedto Admissions Directorate, NUST, Sector H-12, Islamabad, along with written application. Re-checking involves the verification of paper for any unmarked answers and error free totaling of marks.

Q: I am a born Pakistani with a foreign passport; can I apply as an expatriate student?
A: If you don't have Pakistani nationality like having CNIC / NICOP, you can apply as an international student (foreign student). You can apply on the basis of NET or ACT/SAT or both as per eligibility criteria but have to pay tuition fee and allied charges as admissible for foreign nationals.

If you are a POC holder and applying on the basis of NET and ACT / SAT national seat, you will be paying tuition fee and allied charges as admissible for national student.

Q: I am a foreigner and want to get admission at NUST?
A: If you fulfill the eligibility criteria, you can apply for admission on the basis of NET or ACT / SAT (International seat) or both as per policy in vogue.For details, visithttps://nust.edu.pk/admissions/undergraduates/international-students/.

Q: I am an overseas Pakistani who has completed O & A level from abroad, do I have to pass Urdu, Islamiyat / Ethics, and Pakistan Studies to get IBCC equivalence?
A: No, there isn’t any such compulsory requirement of passing the above-mentioned subjects, as per IBCC equivalence rules. For further details, please visit IBCC website:https://ibcc.edu.pk/rules-for-equivalence/

Q: I am in my final year of A Level and my result will be announced in August 2024. Can I apply?
A: Yes, you can apply, as in such case you will be evaluated on the basis of ‘O’ levels equivalence marks but you have to provide equivalence certificate of ‘A’ level within 15 days of announcement of result or at the time of admission.

Q: I could not appear in NUST Entry Test, Can I take the Test on some other day?
A: NUST is conducting NET in paper based format on one day and in computer based format in two sessions daily, on different days, as per the schedule. As per policy, if a candidate misses the Test session he / she is not given another chance. But in case of extreme emergency, requests may be entertained on case to case basis, depending on availability of space.

Q: I have DAE background. Which programmes can I apply for?
A: DAE candidate should have completed DAE with minimum 60% marks in Matric as well as DAEThe detail of Engineering programmes offered to DAE candidates as approved by Pakistan Engineering Council (PEC) can be accessed by visitinghttps://nust.edu.pk/admissions/undergraduates/dae-eligibility-criteria/.

Q: If I am selected on all 3 merits, what should I do?
A: If selected on the basis of NET and ACT / SAT on national as well as international seat, you have the option to retain any one seat of your own choice.

Q: If I have taken ACT / SAT Tests, do I still have to sit in the Entry Test?
A: Separate admission form will be available on NUST website for applying on the basis ofACT / SATandNET.Those applying on ACT / SAT basis does not require to appear in NET, however they can opt for NET in addition to ACT /SAT specified seats. In this way, they will be considered for both the streams.

Q: Is a candidate having less than 60% marks in FSc part-1 eligible to apply for admission in NUST?
A: Yes, the marks entered for FSc part-1 will be utilized for merit generation. However on joining NUST, the candidate must have secure 60% or more marks in FSc complete result.

Q: Is an equivalence certificate of qualification other than Matric & FSc / FA necessary? Can I not submit my O'level and A' level certificates? (I ask so, because I will be applying from abroad)?
A: Equivalence certificates duly issued by IBCC Pakistan are necessary for those candidates who do not possess Matric & FSc / FA qualifications. Submission of only O&A level grade / mark sheets will not serve the purpose. All non FSc stream candidates must have equivalence certificate of their qualification, duly obtained from IBCC, Pakistan in relevant groups/subjects and with minimum 60% marks.

Q: Is migration to NUST allowed?
A: Yes, as per migration policy, students can apply for migration after completion of first year and before the beginning of final year. However, applicants with exceptionally good academic record are considered for migration to NUST. For detailed migration policyclick here.

Q: Is my result of NUMS MDCAT 2023 valid for admission in MBBS (Session 2024-25)?
A: NUMS MDCAT 2023(or previous) will no longer be acceptable for MBBS admissions (Session 2024 - 25).

Q: Is there any age limit for Undergraduate Admission?
A: There is no age limit for Undergraduate Admission.

Q: Is there any installment plan for the payment of tuition fee?
A: As per PM&DC Regulations, a student can pay the tuition fee in installments on aquarterly,six monthlyorannual basis, with a discount of0%on quarterly,2%on six monthly and4%on annual basis. Necessary details will be incorporated in the fee invoice.

Q: Is there any negative marking in the NUST Entry Test?
A: No, there is no negative marking.

Q: Name on my CNIC and Passport is different from that on my academic documents (O / A Level) or equivalence certificate. Which name should I use to apply for admission?
A: ​For this, you have to get your name changed on your CNIC and Passport, because University will use the name as per your Matric or O level equivalence certificate (SSC), duly issued by IBCC.

Q: What category of candidates can apply for admission on the basis of ACT / SAT?
A: Any candidate can apply for admission on the basis of ACT / SAT provided he / she has score for the prescribed disciplines of study. Separate admission form will be available on NUST website for this purpose. Admission on ACT / SAT basis has two categories, i.e National and International. Candidates admitted on National seats pay tuition fee and allied charges in Pak currency whereas candidates admitted against International seats pay the required dues at rates defined for international students. For admission against international seats, the following categories will be eligible:-Pakistani national students having passed an examination equivalent to intermediate level of Pakistan from a foreign education system.Foreigners and Pakistanis having dual nationality, irrespective of the place of their HSSC study, i.e Pakistan or abroad.Pakistani students who studied abroad but have passed the requisite examination from a Pakistani board.

Online registration system does not require attachment of documents / credentials. However, the same will be required for verification during selection and admission process of selected candidates.
